Top donor lists only those who have given $100 or more. ActBlue is an online fundraising platform used by Democratic candidates; WinRed serves the same function for Republican candidates. Contributions listed under these groups are from individual donors who used the platforms to make donations. Candidates do not have to itemize donations of less than $50 in campaign finance filings. Individuals, political action committees and party committees may give up to $500 for each the primary and general election, for a total of $1,000, to traditionally financed candidates. Contributions made to a particular candidate that are earmarked or directed through an intermediary such as a PAC or a party committee are considered contributions to the candidate, and are subject to the contribution limits.

Some candidates in this race have applied to run as a Maine Clean Election Act (MCEA) candidate, choosing to participate in Maine's voluntary public financing system. This means they are funding their campaigns through a capped amount of small initial private donations, or seed money, and then a sum of public funds from the state that is allotted depending on the type of race and whether it is contested. Clean election candidates have a different campaign finance reporting schedule than traditionally financed candidates — seed money reports are due by May 21 — and must go through a certification process before they can receive clean election funding. As such, the status of their publicly reported funds varies. In some cases, you will see seed money donations, in others you may see no funds yet, depending on what has been reported by the Maine Ethics Commission. Candidates will receive a set amount of public financing once they are certified: $675 for an uncontested House primary, $3,325 for a contested House primary, $2,650 for an uncontested Senate primary and $13,250 for a contested Senate primary. Initial seed money is capped at $1,000 for Maine House candidates and $3,000 for Maine Senate candidates. We will update the data once it becomes available from the Maine Ethics Commission.
